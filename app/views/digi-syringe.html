OVERVIEW
  • Adhered a linear potentiometer to interior of syringe barrel to track the movement of the plunger and the volumes of fluid drawn into the syringe.
  • Captured analog input from the resistance of the potentiometer, and mapped it to the draw length of the syringe.
  • Used a drill press and drill machine to alter binding clips so that the hardware could attach to the syringe.
  • Utilized the Serial monitor to report back the total volume plunged.
    • SUMMARY
    In compounding chemotherapy doses, clinicians often have to use a syringe multiple times to withdraw a solution from a single stage. This can lead to miscounting the number of times a solution was withdrawn from the stage, which could have disastrous effects on the patient. To remedy this issue, our team of 5 created a modular device that can be applied onto a syringe to track how often a syringe has been plunged and what total volume has been plunged.
