OVERVIEW
  • Created functional prototype to prevent finger dislocations in child athletes with five person team.
  • Used SolidWorks to produce 3D-printed components from ABS plastic that would limit hyperextension and hyperflexion of the metacarpophalangeal joints (MCP).
  • Developed mathematical models to test the springs utilized in the project.
  • Utilized low and medium-fidelity prototypes as well as concept-sketching to reiterate the design.
    • SUMMARY
    Given the problem space of child safety in sports, our team of 5 decided to tackle the issue of hyperflexion and hyperextension of the phalangeal joints, a problem prominent in hand-ball sports. We pursued a fitted hand-exoskeleton 3D-printed from ABS plastic that would be placed within a sports glove. The glove would limit both flexion and extension while at the same time not impacting the performance of the athlete.
